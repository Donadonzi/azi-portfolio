OVERVIEW

Chef Koochooloo is a web application that teach STEAM to children grades K - 2, through preparing and cooking a dish from different countries in the world. This version of the web app had put the main focus on the students. Through their pilot program and user feedback, Chef Koochooloo realized, they lacked features which would make it more usable for teachers. I and my other 3 teammates were tasked with coming up with design features to make Chef Koochooloo more functional and usable for teachers using the web app.

Problem

There is no teacher portal or dashboard to allow teachers create an educational plan for the class.

Solution

We designed a portal that teachers can customize contents and modules and search in subjects and contents to increase engaging time.

RESEARCH

Interview: 6 Teachers
Every 10-20 minutes
Teachers try to give students breaks or create transitions to maintain student engagement
Adjustable Lessons
Teachers need lessons that are adjustable for the different learning styles and teaching styles
Common Core
Teachers needed to know what Common Core and/or NGSS standards they are teaching

Student Observation

We observed that the students remained focus at the start of the lesson but as the lesson continued with the same activity, they were noticeably distracted. In both classes, this student disengagement, caused the lesson to be rushed at the end.

TARGET AUDIENCE

Teacher who use Chef Koochooloo application as a secondary educational tool in their classes

Teachers tend to

  • Rearrange content within a lesson
  • Personalize lessons on a class by class basis
  • Plans lessons that are inclusive of different learning styles and promote multisensory learning
  • Create structured lesson plans that include many short activities and physical breaks
  • Use are range of materials (analog and digital) to engage students

Teachers frustrated by

  • Spending more than a few hours preparing a new lesson plan
  • Static content I cannot change
  • Using multiple management tools to track student progress

Persona

Teachers

Needs

Adjust lessons content - Create new lesson plan for each class

Goals

Keep students engaged during lessons

FINAL DESIGN

Final Key Functions

Customizability / Edit

Users are able to customize the lessons content based on classes and students needs and other factors

Discoverability / Search

Users are able to search and discover subjects through lessons

Key Pages

1. Edit Lessons

If a teacher decides to edit, for example, the "France" lesson, this is new page where they can do that.


- The lower box in frame displays the tagged teaching content in the lesson.
- The left side of the page shows all the content within each country lesson.
- Selecting any content on the left will fill the preview window in the center of the page.
- Users can drag and drop content on the left to rearrange the lesson to best suit their class' needs.
- Clicking the eyeball on each content box will either include or exclude that piece of content.

2. Search / Homepage

The new homepage when a teacher signs in has a clear search bar to the right. The settings button is moved to the header and the Chef Koochooloo logo replaces a globe icon which also acts as a homepage button.


- Teachers can now search by Common Core standards among other tags like "Fractions."
- Search results display countries that contain what the teacher is searching for.

CONCLUSION

We knew that we weren't only redesigning an aspect of the existing app, but rather creating a new experience from the teachers point of view. We realized that our research was leading us to a design that our stakeholder might not be expecting. As long as we had the data to support our design, we felt comfortable moving forward. All that being said, I feel very confident in our design. I believe we successfully kept our design within the companies brand and style while also shifting the focus more towards the teachers. In our 3 week sprint, I felt our design tackled a good amount of pain points teachers experience not just grades K-5 but even through high school. I am positive our solution will help Chef Koochooloo web application.
