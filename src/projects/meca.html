Meca

Mobile Application Design

  • Product
  • UX
  • UI

A unique entertaining way to introduce public art to whom live or visit San Francisco. On Meca app, users engage with art subjects through the gaming experience.

Tools

Adobe Xd, Illustrator, Photoshop

Team

4 Designers

My Role

Project Coordinator, UX & UI Designer, Survey, Wireframing, Usability Testing

IMPACT

Public arts is free and accessible to everyone. There are tousands of public art pieces in San Francisco. It makes this city a great source of education through an amusement experience.

Problem

There is no comprehensive source for San Francisco residents to find and visit public artworks easily.

Solution

We designed a mobile application that entertain and educate users through AR gaming experience.

FINAL DESIGN

Design Decisions

At the end, the team after a 3-hour meeting decided to go with a general idea. I and Michael tried to create the best understanding UI screens. I tested both result with some friends and Michael’s onboarding had been selected based on 7 quick usability testings.

Final Key Functions

AR Technology

Users are able to pick favorite arts and add into their museum using AR technology through a quick scan and drangging motion gesture

Virtual Museum

Users have personal virtual museum to collect arts. They are able to edit the interior design and the artworks

Before and After

It is possible for users to see how the environment looked prior to when the art installed

1. Sign in/Sign up

 

2. Onboarding How to use


Users will be familiar with the AR instructions and how to use it toward the best result through the game in the future.

They also will get more information about how to use map and discover art around. It also is helpful to understand how to use and see “your collection”

Museum Icon

User see personal museum to edit and modify the space and artworks

1. Friends List

After adding each public art, user see friends list who saved the art work in their musem

2. Map

User scroll down on the page to look at the map

1. Heatpoint

The heat point shows how the user is close to a public art piece

2. Hints

On the map there is a hint about how far the user is from the artwork by walking/driving

3. Map/Location Icon

User see the map to check if there is any close public art to visit. The user can check hints and the locations of artworks on map
